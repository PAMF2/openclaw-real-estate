# Mortgage Underwriting Guidelines Reference
> Common underwriting standards across major loan products. Always verify against current agency guidelines — this reference reflects standard industry positions as of early 2026.

---

## Conventional Loans (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac)

### Loan Limits (2025)
- 1-unit: $806,500 (baseline conforming limit)
- 2-unit: $1,032,650
- 3-unit: $1,248,150
- 4-unit: $1,551,250
- High-cost areas (Alaska, Hawaii, certain metros): up to 150% of baseline

### Credit Score
- Minimum: 620 (most products)
- < 680: increased pricing adjustments (LLPAs), manual review recommended for risk-layered files
- Qualifying score: lowest middle score across all borrowers on the loan

### LTV Matrix (Primary Residence, Purchase)
| Occupancy | Property Type | Max LTV | Min Down |
|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|
| Primary | 1-unit | 97% (DU) | 3% |
| Primary | 2-unit | 85% | 15% |
| Primary | 3-4 unit | 75% | 25% |
| Second Home | 1-unit | 90% | 10% |
| Investment | 1-unit | 85% | 15% |
| Investment | 2-4 unit | 75% | 25% |

### DTI Limits
- DU Approve/Eligible: up to 45% back-end (50% with strong compensating factors per DU)
- LPA Accept: up to 45% (expanded to 50% with strong risk profile)
- Manual underwriting: 36% housing / 45% total (45% housing / 50% total with documented compensating factors)

### PMI Requirements
- Required when LTV > 80%
- Can be lender-paid (LPMI), borrower-paid monthly (BPMI), or single premium
- Cancellable when LTV reaches 80% (federal law — Homeowners Protection Act)
- Automatic termination at 78% LTV based on original amortization schedule

### Reserves (Post-Close)
- Primary 1-unit: 0-2 months PITIA (DU/LPA driven)
- Primary 2-4 unit: 6 months PITIA recommended
- Second home: 2 months PITIA
- Investment: 6 months PITIA per financed property
- Acceptable reserve sources: checking, savings, 60% of vested retirement, 70% of stock/bonds (after liquidation haircut)

### Eligible Assets for Down Payment
- Borrower's own funds (checking, savings, investment accounts)
- Gift funds — relatives per guidelines; gift letter + transfer documentation required
- Employer assistance programs
- Secondary financing (meets guidelines)
- Bridge loans (subject to payoff at closing)

### Employment and Income
- 2-year employment history required (gaps documented and explained)
- Self-employed: 2 years same business, most recent 2 years tax returns
- W-2 wage earner: most recent 30-day paystub + 2 years W-2s
- Verbal VOE within 10 business days of closing (written within 3 business days for certain scenarios)

## FHA Loans (HUD Handbook 4000.1)

### Loan Limits (2025)
- Vary by county — nationwide floor: $524,225 (1-unit)
- Ceiling in high-cost areas: $1,209,750 (1-unit)
- Check HUD's county limit lookup for specific markets

### Minimum Credit Score and Down Payment
| Credit Score | Min Down Payment | AUS |
|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| 580+ | 3.5% | DU/LPA or manual |
| 500-579 | 10% | Manual underwriting only |
| < 500 | Not eligible | — |

### Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP)
**Upfront MIP (UFMIP)**: 1.75% of base loan amount (financed or paid at closing)

**Annual MIP (monthly)**:
| LTV | Term | Annual MIP Rate |
|-----|------|----------------|
| <= 90% | 30yr | 0.50% |
| > 90% | 30yr | 0.55% |
| <= 78% | 15yr | 0.15% |
| > 78%-90% | 15yr | 0.40% |
| > 90% | 15yr | 0.65% |

MIP duration: Loans with LTV > 90% — MIP for life of loan. LTV <= 90% — MIP cancels at 11 years.

### Manual Underwriting Triggers
Manual underwriting required (AUS findings not sufficient) when:
- Credit score < 640
- Any mortgage late in past 12 months
- Chapter 13 bankruptcy discharged < 2 years
- Non-traditional credit profile (no tradelines)
- AUS refers to manual underwriting

**Manual UW Compensating Factors** (needed when DTI > 31/43):
- Verified and documented cash reserves (1-3 months PITIA)
- Minimal increase in housing payment (< $100 or < 5%)
- Residual income meets VA tables
- No discretionary debt (all revolving balances zero)

### Collections and Judgments
- Judgments: must be paid in full prior to or at closing (or payment plan with 12-month history)
- Collections > $2,000: must be paid, payment plan documented, or addressed in manual UW
- Medical collections: excluded from DTI calculation; document in file
- Non-medical collections < $2,000: no action required per 4000.1 (but AUS may factor)

### Waiting Periods (Derogatory Credit)
| Event | FHA Waiting Period | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Foreclosure | 3 years | From deed transfer date |
| Short Sale | 3 years | From closing date |
| Chapter 7 BK | 2 years | From discharge date |
| Chapter 13 BK | 12 months | Satisfactory payment history + court approval |
| DIL | 3 years | From deed transfer |

### Student Loans (FHA)
- Use actual monthly payment per credit report or servicer letter
- If deferred or income-based repayment (IBR) shows $0: use 0.5% of outstanding balance as qualifying payment
- No exception for zero payment under IBR — use the 0.5% rule

### Seller Concessions
Maximum 6% of sales price. Must not exceed actual closing costs.

## VA Loans (VA Lenders Handbook M26-7)

### Key Benefits
- No down payment required (up to full entitlement)
- No monthly mortgage insurance
- Funding fee in lieu of PMI (financed into loan)
- Manual underwriting available with residual income strength

### Funding Fee (2024-2025)
| Down Payment | First Use | Subsequent Use |
|-------------|----------|---------------|
| < 5% | 2.15% | 3.30% |
| 5%-9.99% | 1.50% | 1.50% |
| 10%+ | 1.25% | 1.25% |

Funding fee exemptions: Veterans with service-connected disability rating >= 10%, surviving spouses of veterans who died in service.

### Entitlement and Loan Limits
- Full entitlement (no prior unpaid VA loan): no loan limit, no down payment required
- Reduced/remaining entitlement: calculate bonus entitlement; down payment may be required above county limit
- Verify entitlement via Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — confirm in WebLGY or via borrower copy

### Residual Income (Primary Qualifier)
Residual income = Monthly gross income - all monthly debts - taxes - maintenance allowance

**Minimum Residual Income by Family Size and Region**:

| Family Size | Northeast | Midwest | South | West |
|------------|-----------|---------|-------|------|
| 1 | $450 | $441 | $441 | $491 |
| 2 | $755 | $738 | $738 | $823 |
| 3 | $909 | $889 | $889 | $990 |
| 4 | $1,025 | $1,003 | $1,003 | $1,117 |
| 5 | $1,062 | $1,039 | $1,039 | $1,158 |
| Each add'l | +$80 | +$80 | +$80 | +$80 |

Note: 120% residual income required if loan amount >= $80,000 and family size < 5. If residual income exceeds 120% of the guideline, DTI up to 41% may be approved.

### DTI Guideline
- 41% total back-end is the benchmark
- Residual income is the primary compensating factor — strong residual income can support DTI > 41%
- VA does not have a maximum DTI per se; underwriter judgment with residual income governs

### Occupancy
- Primary residence only for VA purchase loans
- Veteran must certify occupancy intent at closing
- Exceptions: active duty PCS situations — spouse occupancy acceptable

### CAIVRS Clearance
All VA borrowers must be cleared through HUD's Credit Alert Verification Reporting System (CAIVRS) before loan approval — confirms no federal debt delinquency.

## Self-Employed Borrower Documentation

### Definition
A borrower is considered self-employed when they own 25% or more of a business entity.

### Required Documentation (2-Year History)
- Federal personal tax returns (1040) — all pages, all schedules — for 2 most recent years
- Business tax returns based on entity type:
  - Sole Proprietor: Schedule C
  - Partnership: Form 1065 + all K-1s
  - S-Corporation: Form 1120S + all K-1s
  - C-Corporation: Form 1120 (if borrower takes W-2 from corp, review both)
- Year-to-date Profit and Loss statement (within 60 days of closing)
- Business bank statements (2-3 months) to verify business account activity

### Income Calculation Method

**Step 1: Start with net profit (Schedule C) or K-1 ordinary income**

**Step 2: Add back allowable non-cash deductions**:
| Add-Back Item | Form Location | Notes |
|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Depreciation | Sch C / 1065 / 1120S | Full amount |
| Depletion | Sch C | Full amount |
| Business use of home | Sch C line 30 | Full amount |
| Meals and entertainment | Sch C | 50% add-back (50% non-deductible already) |
| Amortization | Sch C | Full amount |
| Non-recurring losses | Various | One-time items only, document |
| Mileage (above actual cost) | Sch C | Calculated excess |

**Step 3: Subtract non-recurring income** (one-time gains, tax refunds, insurance proceeds)

**Step 4: Divide by 24 months** for monthly qualifying income

**Declining Income Rule**: If Year 2 income is more than 20% below Year 1, use Year 2 income only (do not average). If decline is greater than 25%, additional analysis required — business may not be stable enough.

### Business Financial Strength
For S-Corp and Partnership borrowers, confirm the business can support the income withdrawal:
- Business checking account balance should reflect ongoing operations
- No large undocumented cash withdrawals from business accounts
- If business shows a loss in either year, that loss must be factored into income calculation

## Non-QM Products Overview

Non-QM loans do not conform to CFPB Qualified Mortgage rule parameters. No GSE guarantee. Lender retains credit risk or sells to private secondary market.

### Bank Statement Loans
- Uses 12 or 24 months of bank statements in lieu of tax returns
- Personal bank statements: average monthly deposits (typically 50% expense factor applied)
- Business bank statements: average monthly deposits (expense factor varies 10-50% by lender)
- Common use case: self-employed borrowers with significant write-offs reducing taxable income

### Asset Depletion / Asset Dissipation
- Eligible assets (retirement, savings, investment) are divided over a fixed term (e.g., 84 or 120 months) to create a qualifying monthly income
- No employment or income documentation required
- Example: $1,200,000 in eligible assets / 84 months = $14,285 qualifying monthly income

### DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) Loans
- Investment property only — no personal income required
- Qualifying ratio: Property rental income / PITIA
- Typical minimums: DSCR >= 1.0 (break-even), preferred >= 1.20
- Some lenders allow DSCR < 1.0 at higher rates with larger down payment

### ITIN Loans
- For borrowers without a Social Security Number but with an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number
- Typically require 20-30% down, 2-year ITIN tax history, credit tradelines

### Overlays and Lender-Specific Policies
Non-QM guidelines vary significantly by lender. Always confirm:
- Maximum DTI
- Reserve requirements (often 12 months or more)
- Prepayment penalty terms
- Bankruptcy or foreclosure waiting periods (often shorter than agency guidelines)

## Canada: CMHC Insured vs. Conventional

### Insured Mortgages (CMHC, Sagen, Canada Guaranty)
- Required when down payment is less than 20% of purchase price
- Maximum purchase price: $1,499,999 CAD (property above this amount not eligible for default insurance)
- Amortization: maximum 25 years for insured mortgages (30 years available for first-time buyers on new construction as of August 2024)
- Minimum credit score: 600 (lender overlays often higher — 620-680 common)

**CMHC Premium Schedule (% of loan amount)**:
| Down Payment | Premium Rate |
|-------------|-------------|
| 5%-9.99% | 4.00% |
| 10%-14.99% | 3.10% |
| 15%-19.99% | 2.80% |
| 20%+ (conventional) | No insurance required |

### Stress Test (B-20 Guideline)
All federally regulated lenders must qualify borrowers at the higher of:
- The contract mortgage rate + 2.00%, OR
- 5.25% (the regulatory floor as of 2021)

The stress test applies to both insured and uninsured mortgages at federally regulated lenders (banks). Credit unions and some non-bank lenders may operate under provincial rules without the stress test.

### Conventional Mortgages (Canada)
- Down payment >= 20%: mortgage insurance not required
- Maximum amortization: 30 years at most lenders (some allow 35 for certain borrower profiles)
- Stress test still applies at federally regulated lenders

### Self-Employed (Canada)
- Stated income programs available through some lenders and B-lenders
- Traditional qualifying: 2 years T1 Generals (Canadian equivalent of US 1040) + NOA (Notice of Assessment) from CRA
- Line 15000 (total income) used as starting point; add-backs vary by lender
- B-lenders and MIC (Mortgage Investment Corporation) lenders offer alternative income verification programs for self-employed borrowers who cannot qualify at A-lenders

### Key Terminology Differences (Canada vs. US)
| US Term | Canadian Equivalent |
|---------|-------------------|
| Credit Score (FICO) | Beacon Score (Equifax) / Empirica Score (TransUnion) |
| Mortgage Insurance (PMI) | Default Insurance (CMHC/Sagen/Canada Guaranty) |
| Escrow account | Not standard — most Canadians pay property tax directly |
| Property tax impound | Property tax holdback (used in some provinces) |
| Title insurance | Available but not universally required |
| GDS/TDS ratios | Equivalent to front-end / back-end DTI |

**GDS (Gross Debt Service) ratio**: (PITH + 50% condo fees) / gross income — maximum 32% insured, 39% conventional
**TDS (Total Debt Service) ratio**: (PITH + all debts + 50% condo fees) / gross income — maximum 44% insured, 44% conventional
