# Transaction Checklist Templates
> Standard real estate transaction checklists for SkySlope file management. Use these as the baseline for any buyer-side or seller-side transaction. Adjust for state-specific requirements.

---

## Buyer-Side Checklist — Offer to Close

### Phase 1: Pre-Offer
- [ ] Buyer representation agreement signed
- [ ] Proof of funds or pre-approval letter received
- [ ] Buyer consultation completed and documented
- [ ] Buyer agency disclosure delivered and signed
- [ ] Client intake form completed (contact info, goals, timeline)

### Phase 2: Under Contract (Mutual Acceptance)
- [ ] Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA) — fully executed by all parties
- [ ] Addenda — all addenda attached and initialed (inspection, financing, HOA, etc.)
- [ ] Agency Disclosure — signed by buyer
- [ ] Earnest Money Deposit — check or wire received
- [ ] Earnest Money Deposit — confirmed deposited to escrow within contract deadline
- [ ] Escrow receipt from title company — uploaded to SkySlope
- [ ] Mutual acceptance date confirmed and entered into SkySlope

### Phase 3: Inspection Contingency Period
- [ ] Home inspection ordered and scheduled
- [ ] Home inspection report received and delivered to buyer
- [ ] Sewer inspection (if applicable)
- [ ] Roof inspection (if applicable)
- [ ] Septic inspection (if applicable, per contract)
- [ ] Pest/termite inspection (if applicable or required by loan type)
- [ ] Repair Request / Inspection Response form — drafted and sent
- [ ] Seller's response to repair request received
- [ ] Inspection contingency removal signed by buyer (or notice to terminate if applicable)
- [ ] Mutual agreement on repairs or seller credit documented

### Phase 4: Financing Contingency Period
- [ ] Loan application submitted (within 3-5 business days of MEC typically)
- [ ] Loan Estimate received by buyer (TRID — within 3 business days of application)
- [ ] Appraisal ordered by lender
- [ ] Appraisal report received
- [ ] Appraisal at or above purchase price — confirmed
- [ ] If appraisal gap: addendum or renegotiation documented
- [ ] Loan commitment / conditional approval letter received
- [ ] All lender conditions satisfied and documented
- [ ] Financing contingency removal signed by buyer

### Phase 5: Title and Escrow
- [ ] Preliminary title report / title commitment received
- [ ] Title report reviewed by buyer (and attorney in attorney states)
- [ ] Title exceptions reviewed and approved
- [ ] Title objection deadline (if applicable) — tracked
- [ ] Homeowner's insurance policy bound
- [ ] HOA documents received and reviewed (if applicable)
- [ ] HOA estoppel letter / resale certificate received (if required)
- [ ] Property survey (if applicable or required)

### Phase 6: Pre-Closing (Final 10 Days)
- [ ] Closing Disclosure received (TRID — 3 business day minimum before closing)
- [ ] Closing Disclosure reviewed and buyer questions answered
- [ ] Final walkthrough scheduled
- [ ] Final walkthrough completed — property condition confirmed
- [ ] Final walkthrough form signed (if required)
- [ ] Wire instructions confirmed via phone call to title company (never by email)
- [ ] Closing funds wired or cashier's check arranged
- [ ] Closing appointment confirmed — date, time, location

### Phase 7: Closing and Post-Close
- [ ] Buyer signs all closing documents
- [ ] Loan documents signed and notarized
- [ ] Funding confirmation received from lender
- [ ] Deed recorded — recording number documented
- [ ] Keys transferred to buyer
- [ ] Garage openers, mailbox keys, access codes delivered
- [ ] Post-closing package sent to buyer (recorded deed copy, HUD/CD, insurance info)
- [ ] SkySlope file submitted for compliance review
- [ ] Closing gift sent or scheduled
- [ ] Review request sent (5-7 days post-close)

## Seller-Side Checklist — Listing to Close

### Phase 1: Pre-Listing
- [ ] Listing agreement signed — all pages initialed
- [ ] Seller's Disclosure Statement / Property Condition Report — completed and signed
- [ ] Lead-based paint disclosure (required for homes built before 1978)
- [ ] Agency Disclosure — delivered and signed
- [ ] Natural hazard disclosure (state-specific — e.g., California NHD)
- [ ] HOA disclosure documents delivered (if applicable)
- [ ] Local transfer disclosure (city/county — state-specific)
- [ ] CMA / pricing analysis documented
- [ ] Estimated seller net sheet provided
- [ ] Listing input sheet completed for MLS
- [ ] Professional photos scheduled and completed
- [ ] Marketing approval from seller documented (virtual tour, social media, etc.)

### Phase 2: Offer Review and Negotiation
- [ ] All offers received uploaded to SkySlope
- [ ] Counter offers documented with all redlines preserved
- [ ] Final executed Purchase and Sale Agreement — all pages uploaded
- [ ] All addenda uploaded and confirmed complete
- [ ] Mutual acceptance date confirmed in SkySlope

### Phase 3: Under Contract — Contingency Period
- [ ] Earnest money deposit confirmed received by escrow
- [ ] Escrow receipt uploaded to SkySlope
- [ ] Inspection access coordinated with seller (date, lockbox, utilities on)
- [ ] Repair request received and reviewed with seller
- [ ] Seller response to repair request — executed and uploaded
- [ ] Any agreed credits or repair addenda executed
- [ ] Inspection contingency removal received from buyer

### Phase 4: Appraisal and Financing
- [ ] Appraisal appointment scheduled
- [ ] Appraiser access coordinated with seller (or listing agent present)
- [ ] Appraisal value confirmed at or above purchase price
- [ ] If low appraisal: renegotiation or appraisal gap addendum documented
- [ ] Buyer's loan commitment received (if required per contract)

### Phase 5: Title and Pre-Closing
- [ ] Title search ordered
- [ ] Title issues resolved (liens, easements, old mortgages) — documentation uploaded
- [ ] Payoff statement(s) received from seller's mortgage lender(s)
- [ ] HOA payoff or transfer fee confirmed (if applicable)
- [ ] Utilities scheduled for disconnect/transfer on closing date
- [ ] Final walkthrough date confirmed with buyer's agent

### Phase 6: Closing Prep
- [ ] Closing Disclosure reviewed with seller
- [ ] Seller net sheet (final) provided before closing
- [ ] Closing appointment confirmed — date, time, location
- [ ] Mobile notary arranged (if seller not attending in person)
- [ ] Keys, garage openers, mailbox keys, alarm codes prepared for transfer

### Phase 7: Closing and Post-Close
- [ ] All closing documents signed by seller
- [ ] Deed executed and notarized
- [ ] Deed recorded — recording number confirmed
- [ ] Funds disbursed to seller
- [ ] Payoff(s) confirmed satisfied
- [ ] SkySlope file submitted for compliance review
- [ ] Thank-you sent to seller
- [ ] Review request sent (5-7 days post-close)

## Key Deadlines and Typical Timeframes

The following are typical timeframes in a standard 30-45 day residential transaction. Actual deadlines are governed by the executed contract — always verify against the specific PSA.

| Milestone | Typical Timeframe from MEC |
|---|---|
| Earnest money deposited | 1-3 business days |
| Home inspection completed | 5-10 calendar days |
| Repair request submitted | Within inspection contingency period |
| Loan application submitted | 3-5 business days |
| Loan Estimate delivered to buyer | 3 business days from application (TRID) |
| Appraisal ordered | 7-10 days from MEC |
| Appraisal received | 2-3 weeks from MEC (varies by market) |
| Title commitment received | 10-15 days from MEC |
| HOA documents delivered | 5-10 days (varies by state) |
| Financing contingency removal | 21-30 days from MEC (per contract) |
| Closing Disclosure delivered | 3 business days before closing (TRID) |
| Final walkthrough | 1-3 days before closing |
| Closing funds wired | 1 business day before closing (varies) |
| Closing date | Per contract (typically 30-45 days from MEC) |

## Common Missing Document Flags

The following documents are the most frequently missing or incomplete in SkySlope files at compliance review. Use this list to pre-audit any file before submission.

### Almost Always Flagged
- **Agency Disclosure**: Missing buyer or seller signature. Must be signed before writing or accepting any offer.
- **Earnest Money Receipt**: Uploaded late or missing. Required within 2-3 business days of deposit.
- **Inspection Contingency Removal**: Frequently missing from buyer-side files. Required even if buyer is satisfied with inspection — removal must be in writing.
- **Lead-Based Paint Disclosure**: Missing on pre-1978 homes. Federal law — non-negotiable.
- **All Addenda Fully Initialed**: Individual pages missing initials from one or more parties.

### Frequently Flagged
- **HOA Documents and Acknowledgment**: Delivered but buyer signature on receipt missing.
- **Seller's Disclosure (all pages)**: Incomplete responses or missing signature on final page.
- **Final Executed Repair Addendum**: Verbal or email agreements not reduced to a written addendum.
- **Financing Contingency Removal**: Missing or received after the contract deadline — note the date discrepancy.
- **Closing Disclosure (buyer copy)**: Not uploaded to buyer-side file. TC must confirm CD was received by buyer at least 3 business days prior.
- **Lender Commitment Letter**: Referenced in contract but not uploaded to file.

### Occasionally Flagged
- **Natural Hazard Disclosure (California)**: Third-party report not attached to listing file.
- **Well and Septic Inspection**: Required by contract but report missing from file.
- **HOA Estoppel / Resale Certificate**: Required in many states before closing — frequently late.
- **Wire Confirmation**: Proof of wire receipt from title not uploaded to post-close file.
- **MLS printout at closing price**: Required in some brokerage compliance policies.
