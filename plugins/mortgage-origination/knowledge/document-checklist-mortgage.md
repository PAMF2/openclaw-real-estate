# Mortgage Document Checklist
> Standard document requirements for residential mortgage origination. Applies to purchase, refinance, switch/transfer, and renewal transactions. Adjust per lender guidelines and jurisdiction.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

Match the borrower profile and deal type to identify required documents. Items marked with an expiry window must be re-collected if they fall outside that window before closing.

**Expiry reference:**
- Pay stubs: 30 days
- Employment letters: 90 days (some lenders require 30 days)
- Bank statements: 90 days
- NOA: 2 most recent tax years
- Credit bureau: 120 days
- Appraisal: 90-180 days (lender-dependent)

---

## 1. Identity Verification

Required for all borrowers and co-borrowers.

| Document | Details | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Government-issued photo ID | Passport, driver's licence, or equivalent | Two pieces if one is non-photo |
| Secondary ID | Credit card, health card, SIN card (do not photocopy SIN) | Not required by all lenders |

**FINTRAC / AML Note (Canada):** Brokers must verify identity per FINTRAC requirements before submitting. Acceptable methods: in-person, dual-process, or credit file method.

---

## 2. Employment Verification

### 2a. Salaried / Hourly Employees

| Document | Expiry | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Employment letter on company letterhead | 90 days | Must confirm: employer name, position, start date, income (base + bonus/overtime if used), employment type (full-time/part-time/contract) |
| Two most recent pay stubs | 30 days | Must match income stated in employment letter |
| T4 slips (Canada) / W-2 forms (US) — 2 years | None | Required when bonus, overtime, or commission is used for qualification |
| Year-to-date (YTD) earnings statement | 30 days | Required when income has changed or is variable |

**Notes:**
- Probationary employees: most lenders require probation to be complete or confirmed unconditional
- Contract employees: some lenders require minimum 2-year contract history and contract renewal letter

### 2b. Self-Employed Borrowers

| Document | Expiry | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| T1 General — 2 most recent years (Canada) | None | Full return including all schedules |
| Notice of Assessment (NOA) — 2 years (Canada) | None | Must match T1; confirm no amounts owing to CRA |
| Business financials — 2 years | None | Prepared by CPA or accountant; some lenders accept internally prepared with CPA letter |
| Business licence or articles of incorporation | None | Confirms active business and ownership |
| CRA My Account print (Canada) | 30 days | Some lenders accept as NOA substitute if recent |
| IRS Tax Returns (Form 1040 + all schedules) — 2 years (US) | None | Self-employed: also provide Schedule C, K-1 |
| Business tax returns (Form 1120 / 1120S) — 2 years (US) | None | Required for corporation or S-corp owners |
| YTD profit and loss statement | 30 days | Internally prepared or CPA-prepared; required by most Alt-A and B lenders |

**Notes:**
- Income used = 2-year average of line 15000 (Canada) or Schedule C net income (US) — after add-backs for depreciation, depletion, business use of home
- If business income has declined more than 25% year-over-year, most lenders use the lower year

### 2c. Rental Income

| Document | Expiry | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Current lease agreement(s) | None | Signed by tenant; confirm term and monthly rent |
| 12 months rental history (bank statements or deposit receipts) | None | Confirms rent is being received |
| T776 Rental Income Schedule (Canada) — 2 years | None | Required for existing rental properties |
| Schedule E (US) — 2 years | None | Required for existing rental properties |
| Property tax bill for rental property | None | Confirms ownership and annual tax amount |

**Notes:**
- Offset method (Canada): rental income added to gross income, full PITH of rental added to debt obligations
- Rental offset method: most lenders allow 50-80% of rental income to offset the rental property's carrying cost instead of adding both sides

### 2d. Other Income Sources

| Income Type | Document Required |
|------------|------------------|
| Pension / CPP / OAS | Annual pension statement or letter from pension administrator |
| Employment Insurance (EI) | Statement of benefits; most lenders do not qualify on EI unless seasonal and historical |
| Child/spousal support (income) | Court order or separation agreement + 12 months bank statements confirming receipt |
| Child/spousal support (obligation) | Court order or separation agreement; full obligation included in TDS/back-end DTI |
| Investment income | 2 years T5/1099-DIV; 2-year average used |
| Foreign income | Foreign tax returns, employer letter in English/French, bank statements confirming deposit |

## 3. Down Payment Verification

All down payment funds must be verified. Gifted funds and borrowed funds follow different rules.

### 3a. Own Funds (Savings / Investments)

| Document | Expiry | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Bank statements — 90 days | 90 days | Must show borrower's name, account number, all transactions; large deposits require sourcing |
| Investment account statements | 90 days | RRSP, TFSA, RRIF, FHSA, 401k, IRA, brokerage accounts |
| Proceeds of sale letter | N/A | If down payment comes from another property sale; confirm with lawyer/notary statement |

**Large deposit rule:** Any non-payroll deposit greater than 25% of the borrower's gross monthly income (or lender-specific threshold) must be explained and sourced with documentation.

### 3b. Gifted Down Payment

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Gift letter (signed by donor) | Must confirm: amount, relationship to borrower, that it is a non-repayable gift with no expectation of repayment |
| Proof of donor's ability to give | Bank statement from donor (some lenders require this) |
| Confirmation funds received | Bank statement showing deposit into borrower's account |

**Canada insured mortgages:** Gift must be from an immediate family member (parent, sibling, grandparent, child). Third-party gifts require lender-specific approval.
**US conventional:** Gift from family member acceptable. FHA: wide range of acceptable gift sources.

### 3c. FHSA / RRSP First Home Buyers' Plan (Canada)

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| FHSA contribution room statement | Confirms available balance |
| RRSP statement — 90 days | Confirm funds have been on deposit 90+ days before withdrawal |
| HBP Withdrawal form (T1036) | Completed at time of withdrawal |

---

## 4. Property Documents

### 4a. Purchase Transaction

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Signed purchase and sale agreement (PSA) | Including all schedules and amendments; all parties must have signed |
| MLS listing or property data sheet | Confirms address, legal description, property type, square footage |
| Realtor representation agreement | Required by some lenders |
| Property tax bill or assessment | Confirms annual taxes; used for ratio calculation |

### 4b. Appraisal

| Document | Expiry | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Full appraisal report | 90-180 days | Required for conventional mortgages (LTV > 20%); must be ordered through lender-approved AMC |
| Desktop or drive-by appraisal | Lender-specific | Some lenders accept for straightforward properties |
| AVM (Automated Value Model) | Lender-specific | Accepted by some lenders for refinances and renewals |

### 4c. Condo / Strata-Specific Documents

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Status certificate (Ontario) | Ordered from condo corporation; valid 10 days; reviewed by buyer's lawyer |
| Form B Information Certificate (BC) | Equivalent to status certificate |
| Estoppel certificate (other provinces) | Similar disclosure document |
| Condo questionnaire (lender form) | Completed by condo corporation or property management |
| Meeting minutes — 2 most recent AGMs | Flags special assessments or structural concerns |
| Reserve fund study | Some lenders require if building is 15+ years old |
| Declaration and bylaws | Required by some lenders |
| HOA disclosure package (US) | Fannie Mae and FHA have specific condo approval requirements |

**Key condo risk flags:**
- Special assessment outstanding or pending
- Reserve fund below 10% of annual budget (some lenders require 10% minimum)
- Litigation involving the condo corporation
- Less than 51% owner-occupied (affects insured financing eligibility)
- Single entity owning more than 10% of units

### 4d. Refinance / Switch / Transfer

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Current mortgage statement | Confirms balance, rate, maturity date, lender |
| Current property tax bill | Confirms no arrears |
| Existing appraisal (if recent and lender-accepted) | Within lender's acceptable window |
| Property tax receipt showing no arrears | Some lenders require confirmation taxes are current |

### 4e. New Construction / Pre-Construction

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Builder purchase agreement | Full agreement with all schedules |
| Builder floor plan and specifications | Required for appraisal |
| Confirmation of deposit paid | Bank statement or receipt |
| Occupancy date / closing date confirmation | Most recent update from builder |
| Builder's warranty confirmation | Tarion (Ontario), BC Home Owner Protection Office, etc. |

---

## 5. Liability and Credit Verification

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Credit bureau (pulled by lender or broker) | 120 days; must reflect current obligations |
| Explanation letter for derogatory items | For collections, judgments, late payments; borrower-signed |
| Bankruptcy / Consumer Proposal discharge | If applicable; full discharge documents; confirm waiting period met |
| Divorce / separation agreement | Required if child/spousal support is an obligation; used in TDS/back-end DTI |
| Property tax arrears confirmation | Tax arrears must be cleared before or at closing |
| CRA statement showing no amounts owing | Required by many lenders for self-employed borrowers |

---

## 6. Closing Documents (Broker Reference)

These are collected or confirmed by the real estate lawyer / notary at closing. Provided here for broker awareness.

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Mortgage commitment letter (signed) | Signed by borrower confirming acceptance of lender terms |
| Void cheque or pre-authorized debit form | For mortgage payment setup |
| Insurance binder or certificate | Home/fire insurance effective on closing date; lender named as first loss payee |
| Condo insurance certificate | Proof of unit owner's insurance |
| Bridge financing confirmation (if applicable) | If closing date of purchase precedes proceeds from sale |

---

## Document Collection Priority Order

When gathering documents for a new file, collect in this order to unblock qualification analysis first:

1. ID verification (both borrowers)
2. Income documents (employment letter + pay stubs; or 2yr NOA + T1 for self-employed)
3. Purchase agreement and property tax bill
4. Bank statements for down payment (90 days)
5. Credit bureau (pulled by broker/lender)
6. Condo documents if applicable
7. Appraisal (ordered after approval in principle)
8. Remaining closing conditions
