## 2. FIRST-TIME BUYER'S GUIDE

### The Home Buying Process -- Your Step-by-Step Roadmap

**You are here: PRE-APPROVED AND READY TO SEARCH**

```
STEP 1: GET PRE-APPROVED ................ DONE
  You're pre-approved with Chase for up to $400,000.
  Your pre-approval is typically valid for 60-90 days.
  If we haven't found a home by then, we'll renew it 
  (quick and easy).

STEP 2: DEFINE YOUR SEARCH .............. THIS WEEK
  Complete the needs assessment below so I can set up 
  your custom MLS search. You'll get email alerts as 
  soon as new listings hit in Westside that match your 
  criteria.

STEP 3: TOUR HOMES ...................... WEEKS 1-6
  Average first-time buyer sees 8-12 homes. Some find 
  the one on showing #3, others on #15. No pressure.
  
  What to do during showings:
  - Take photos of each home (helps you remember later)
  - Note your gut reaction within 30 seconds of walking in
  - Open closets, cabinets, and look behind doors
  - Check water pressure, light switches, windows
  - Walk the neighborhood and check at different times of day

STEP 4: MAKE AN OFFER ................... WHEN READY
  When you find the one, we'll move fast:
  - I'll research recent comparable sales
  - We'll discuss offer strategy (price, contingencies, 
    earnest money, closing timeline)
  - I'll prepare the offer and present it to the listing agent
  - Typical earnest money: $4,000-$8,000 (1-2% of price)
  
  What if there are multiple offers?
  - I'll advise you on how to make yours stand out
  - We'll discuss escalation clauses, appraisal gaps, 
    and other competitive strategies
  - You'll never be pressured to exceed your comfort zone

STEP 5: HOME INSPECTION ................. DAYS 1-10
  Cost: $350-$500 (you pay upfront, not rolled into loan)
  Duration: 2-3 hours (I recommend you attend)
  
  NEVER skip the inspection. It's your chance to find out 
  what you're really buying. Common findings:
  - Minor: cosmetic issues, maintenance items
  - Moderate: aging HVAC, roof wear, plumbing issues
  - Major: structural problems, water damage, mold, 
    electrical hazards
  
  After the report, I'll help you decide what to negotiate 
  (repairs, credits, or price reduction) and what's normal 
  wear and tear.

STEP 6: APPRAISAL ....................... DAYS 10-21
  Your lender orders this to confirm the home is worth 
  what you're paying. You pay for it ($400-$700) but the 
  lender chooses the appraiser.
  
  If the appraisal comes in at or above your offer: great.
  If it comes in low: we negotiate. Options include seller 
  reducing price, splitting the difference, or you making 
  up the gap in cash.

STEP 7: LOAN PROCESSING ................. DAYS 7-35
  Chase will be processing your file during this time.
  
  CRITICAL: Do NOT do any of the following between now 
  and closing:
    - Open new credit cards
    - Finance furniture, a car, or anything else
    - Change jobs or employment status
    - Make large deposits without documentation
    - Co-sign any loans
    - Close any credit accounts
  
  Any of these can delay or kill your loan.

STEP 8: FINAL WALKTHROUGH ............... DAY BEFORE CLOSING
  We'll walk through the home one final time to verify:
  - Agreed repairs are completed
  - No new damage has occurred
  - All fixtures and appliances are present
  - The home is in the condition you agreed to buy

STEP 9: CLOSING DAY ..................... TARGET: [DATE ~3 months out]
  Duration: 1-2 hours
  
  What to bring:
  - Government-issued photo ID
  - Cashier's check or wire confirmation for closing costs
  - Any documents your lender requested
  
  What you'll sign:
  - Mortgage note (your promise to repay)
  - Deed of trust (gives lender security interest)
  - Closing disclosure (final accounting of all costs)
  - Various affidavits and disclosures
  
  After signing: you get the keys. You're a homeowner.

STEP 10: MOVE IN ........................ SAME DAY OR NEXT
  - Transfer utilities into your name
  - Change locks (first thing)
  - File change of address with USPS
  - Update address with employer, bank, insurance, DMV
  - Schedule any move-in projects you've planned
  - Take a deep breath -- you did it!
```
