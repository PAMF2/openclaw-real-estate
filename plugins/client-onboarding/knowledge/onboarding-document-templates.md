### 2. Buyer's Guide Outline

**Section 1: The Home Buying Process**
```
Step 1: Get Pre-Approved (1-3 days)
  - What it means vs. pre-qualification
  - Documents you'll need (pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements)
  - Why it matters in competitive offers

Step 2: Define Your Search Criteria (with your agent)
  - Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves vs. deal-breakers
  - Neighborhoods to target
  - Budget reality check (purchase price vs. monthly payment)

Step 3: Search and Tour Homes (1-8 weeks typical)
  - How I find properties (MLS, off-market, coming soon)
  - Showing protocol and feedback
  - How many homes most buyers see before deciding

Step 4: Make an Offer (1-3 days)
  - Offer strategy and terms
  - Earnest money
  - Contingencies explained (inspection, appraisal, financing)
  - What happens if there are multiple offers

Step 5: Under Contract (30-45 days typical)
  - Home inspection (Days 1-10)
  - Appraisal (Days 10-21)
  - Loan processing and underwriting (Days 7-35)
  - Title search and insurance
  - Final walkthrough (Day before or day of closing)

Step 6: Closing Day
  - What to bring
  - What to expect
  - When you get the keys

Step 7: Move In
  - Utility transfers
  - Change of address checklist
  - First 30 days in your new home
```

**Section 2: Understanding Costs**
```
Upfront Costs:
  - Down payment: 3-20% of purchase price
  - Earnest money deposit: 1-3% (applied to down payment)
  - Home inspection: $300-$600
  - Appraisal: $400-$700
  - Closing costs: 2-5% of loan amount

Monthly Costs:
  - Mortgage (principal + interest)
  - Property taxes
  - Homeowner's insurance
  - PMI (if down payment < 20%)
  - HOA (if applicable)
  - Utilities
  - Maintenance reserve (1% of home value per year)
```

**Section 3: Tips for a Smooth Purchase**
```
DO:
  - Stay in close contact with your lender
  - Respond promptly to document requests
  - Keep your current job and income stable
  - Save for closing costs beyond your down payment
  - Ask questions -- there are no dumb ones

DON'T:
  - Open new credit cards or take on new debt
  - Make large purchases (car, furniture) before closing
  - Change jobs during the process
  - Move money between accounts without telling your lender
  - Co-sign loans for anyone
  - Skip the home inspection
```

### 10. Stage-by-Stage Expectations (Buyer)

```
WHAT TO EXPECT AT EACH STAGE

STAGE 1: PRE-APPROVAL (Days 1-5)
  What happens: Lender reviews your finances and issues 
  pre-approval letter.
  Your role: Gather documents, respond to lender requests quickly.
  My role: Connect you with recommended lenders, confirm letter.
  Timeline: 1-5 business days.

STAGE 2: HOME SEARCH (Weeks 1-8+)
  What happens: We tour homes, refine criteria, find the one.
  Your role: Provide feedback, stay available for showings.
  My role: Send listings, schedule showings, advise on value.
  Timeline: Average buyer sees 8-12 homes over 4-8 weeks.

STAGE 3: MAKING AN OFFER (Days 1-3)
  What happens: We craft and submit your offer.
  Your role: Decide on price, terms, and contingencies with 
  my guidance.
  My role: Research comps, advise on strategy, negotiate.
  Timeline: Typically 1-3 days from "I love it" to accepted offer.

STAGE 4: INSPECTION (Days 1-10 after contract)
  What happens: Professional inspector examines the property.
  Your role: Attend inspection (recommended), review report.
  My role: Negotiate repairs or credits based on findings.
  Timeline: Inspection within 7-10 days of accepted offer.

STAGE 5: APPRAISAL (Days 10-21)
  What happens: Lender orders appraisal to confirm home value.
  Your role: Wait (this is between lender and appraiser).
  My role: Provide comps to support value if needed.
  Timeline: 7-14 days after ordering.

STAGE 6: LOAN PROCESSING (Days 7-35)
  What happens: Underwriter reviews your full file.
  Your role: Respond immediately to any document requests. 
  Do NOT change your financial situation.
  My role: Monitor progress, coordinate with lender.
  Timeline: 2-4 weeks.

STAGE 7: CLEAR TO CLOSE (3-5 days before closing)
  What happens: Lender confirms loan is approved and ready.
  Your role: Review closing disclosure, wire funds, get 
  cashier's check.
  My role: Confirm all conditions met, schedule final walkthrough.

STAGE 8: FINAL WALKTHROUGH (Day before or day of closing)
  What happens: We walk through the home to confirm condition.
  Your role: Verify repairs completed, home is in agreed condition.
  My role: Document any issues, negotiate resolution if needed.

STAGE 9: CLOSING DAY
  What happens: Sign documents, transfer funds, get keys.
  Your role: Bring ID, funds, and patience (lots of signatures).
  My role: Review documents, ensure everything is correct.
  Timeline: 1-2 hours at the closing table.

STAGE 10: YOU'RE A HOMEOWNER
  What happens: Move in!
  Your role: Transfer utilities, change address, enjoy.
  My role: Provide move-in checklist, remain available for 
  any post-closing questions.
```
